ALPHA PHI OMEGA

Chi Chapter
University of California

Los Angeles, California

 

 

CONSTITUTION

 

 

PREAMBLE

 

This Constitution, together with the National Alpha Phi Omega Standard Chapter Articles of Association shall set forth the rules, ideas, and procedures with which this Service Fraternity shall be operated.

 

 

ARTICLE I: NAME

 

The name of this organization shall be the Chi Chapter of Alpha Phi Omega.

 

 

ARTICLE II: PURPOSE & PRINCIPLES

The purpose of this Chapter shall be to assemble those students who have an earnest desire to render service to others, are guided by the principles of the Scout Oath and Law, and believe and support the ideas of freedom and democracy.  No Members of this Chapter shall participate in hazing of any nature to any fellow Member or Pledge.

 

This Chapter shall be conducted as a Service Fraternity in accordance with the ideas, policies, and provisions set forth in the National Articles of Incorporation, National Bylaws, and the Standard Chapter Articles of Association of Alpha Phi Omega.

 

 

ARTICLE III: MEMBERSHIP

 

Section 1: Open Membership

 

Clause 1: Membership in this Chapter shall be open to all students, regardless of race, creed, color, religion, gender, ethnicity, sexual orientation, or physical ability, irrespective of their course of study or other fraternal affiliation.

 

Clause 2: Membership in this Chapter shall be granted only after approval of the Chapter and after the applicant has completed the Membership requirements prescribed by the National Fraternity and by this Chapter.

 

 

 

 

Section 2: Pledgeship

 

Clause 1: Pledgeship in this Chapter shall be conferred upon students enrolled at the University of California, Los Angeles who are selected to participate in a period of preparation and training in the ideas and purposes of Alpha Phi Omega.

 

Clause 2: This Chapter shall determine the content and duration of the Pledge term in accordance with the Articles of Incorporation, the National Bylaws, and the operating policies of the National Fraternity.

 

Clause 3: Pledges shall participate in the Pledge Ceremony which shall be conducted according to the official ritual of the National Fraternity.

 

Section 3: Active Membership

 

Clause 1: Active Membership in this Chapter shall be conferred upon students of University of California at Los Angeles who support the principles of Alpha Phi Omega as embodied in the Scout Oath and Law.

 

Clause 2: All new Members of this Chapter must have fulfilled a period of Pledgeship as prescribed in the Chi requirements and must go through an Active initiation ceremony.

 

Clause 3: All initiates shall be considered Active Members.  This status will be maintained by participating in the activities of this Fraternity, giving unselfishly of one’s time and energy to build a program of service, keeping in mind the solemn oath which was taken upon Pledge initiation into Alpha Phi Omega, fulfilling Active requirements, and paying all Dues, fees and assessments by the specified dates.

 

Clause 4: Active Membership shall continue so long as a Member meets Active requirements stated above, does not become an Associate Member by choice or assignment, and is an enrolled graduate or undergraduate student at University of California, Los Angeles.

 

Clause 5: Any Active Member who does not fulfill the Active requirements will be assigned Associate Membership for the subsequent term.

 

Clause 6: Active Members and Pledges may vote upon Chapter business.  Only Actives may run for the office of President, Pledge Parent, or Membership Vice President.  Pledges must “co-VP” with an Active for Fellowship Vice President or Service Vice President.  Both Actives and Pledges in good standing may run for

Administrative Vice President, Finance Vice President, Historian, Sergeant at Arms, or Interchapter Chair.

 

Clause 7: All Active Members shall hold to at least 1 position of leadership.

 

Section 4: Associate Membership

 

Clause 1: This Chapter may confer Associate Membership upon any Active Member who finds it necessary to cease full involvement in Chapter affairs because of other commitments of time and effort, and who informs the Chapter Membership Vice President of his or her desire for such Membership.

 

Clause 2: An Associate Member shall be required to pay Associate Chapter dues, and will have all privileges of Active Membership except for voting, running for office, and holding office.

 

Clause 3: If an Associate Member chose to be an Associate Member at the beginning of a given term, then reinstatement to Active status shall be accomplished by informing the Chapter Membership Vice President.  From that point forward, the Member will be considered an Active Member.

 

Section 5: Inactive Status

 

Failure to fulfill any requirements for Active Membership or Associate Membership shall result in Inactive status and shall revoke privileges of Active Members as stated in Article III, Section 3, or privileges of Associate Membership as stated in Article III, Section 4.  Reinstatement shall be made by informing the Chapter Membership Vice President and completing all necessary requirements as listed.

 

Section 6: Advisory Membership

 

This Chapter may confer Advisory Membership upon persons approved by the Chapter.  Advisor shall have all privileges of Active Membership except for voting and holding office (except as Chairman of the Advisory Committee).  Such Membership may not be conferred upon undergraduate students.

 

Section 7: Honorary Membership

 

This Chapter may confer Honorary Membership upon persons approved by 2/3 vote of the Chapter.  Honorary Members shall have all privileges of Active Membership except for voting and holding office.  Honorary Membership may not be granted to undergraduate students of this university.

 

Section 8: Alumni Membership

 

This Chapter shall confer Alumni Membership to Active or associate Members who leave this Chapter and university.  Alumni shall have all the privileges of Active Membership except for voting and holding office.

 

 

 

Section 9: Membership Transfer

 

This Chapter may accept the transfer into Chi of any Member from another Alpha Phi Omega Chapter.

 

 

ARTICLE IV: OFFICERS & ADVISORS

 

Section 1: The elected officers of this Chapter shall be President, Service Vice President, Membership Vice President, Fellowship Vice President, Pledge Parent, Administrative Vice President, Finance Vice President, Historian, Sergeant-at-Arms, and Interchapter Chair.  The hierarchy of the Chapter officers shall be the order as listed in this section.

 

Section 2: All elected officers shall do their best to serve for their term of 15 weeks or partial term following their election.

 

Section 3: All Chapter officers and Advisors shall be installed according to the installation ceremony provided by the National Fraternity.

 

Section 4: Each officer and Advisor shall be responsible for the training of the successor and compiling and turning over all records and materials of their office in an up-to-date condition within 3 weeks of the new term.

 

Section 5: Chapter officers may be impeached for misconduct or failure to perform their duties by ¾ majority of the Active Members of the Chapter, and only when the proposed charges have been presented at an Active meeting and to the Advisory Committee Chairperson at least 7 days prior to the Active meeting at which the vote will be taken.  All Members should be notified that the vote is to be taken.  An officer so impeached shall be immediately removed from office and a special election shall be scheduled as stated below.

 

Section 6: In the event of a vacancy, a special election shall be held to fill the vacant office.  Until the office is filled, the President may appoint a temporary officer.  If the office of the President is vacant, the next highest offices shall fill the vacancy until the special election may be held.  The special election must take place within 2 weeks unless approval of the Advisory Committee is granted because of special circumstances.

 

Section 7: Duties of Officers include (but are not limited to) the following:

 

Clause 1: President

 

1.      To serve as the presiding officer at regular and special Chapter Meetings, meetings of the Executive Committee, and to serve as a Member of the Advisory Committee.

2.      To coordinate the efforts of all other Chapter officers.

3.      To appoint officers or establish ad-hoc committees as stated in the Constitution.

4.      To serve as an ex-officio Member of all committees.

5.      To see that all National and Chapter policies are followed and that the Chapter is operated according to the National standards and Chapter Constitution and Bylaws.

6.      To serve as chief spokesperson and primary representative of the Chi Chapter.

7.      To have possession of the gavel and bring it to all regular Chapter Meetings.

8.      To approve the treasurer’s choice of chairman to the leadership development fund.

9.      To carry out Chapter correspondence with National, Regional, and Sectional staff, Chapter alumni, and other Chapters.

 

Clause 2: Service Vice President(s)

 

1.      To serve as chairperson(s) of the Service Committee.

2.      To appoint a liaison to facilitate communication with and advise the Pledge Class Service Committee.

3.      To appoint a service project chairperson for each service event.

4.      To prepare a service term report for the next Service Vice President.

 

Clause 3: Membership Vice President(s)

 

1.      To serve as chairperson(s) of the Membership Committee.

2.      To serve as chief liaison to the National Office.

3.      To work with the President to promote and facilitate Chapter involvement in inter-Chapter, sectional, regional, and National Fraternity events.

4.      To assist the Pledge Parent and President to ensure that all National and Chapter pledging standards are met.

5.      To prepare a term record to be used for reference by future Membership Vice Presidents.

 

Clause 4: Fellowship Vice President(s)

 

1.      To serve as chairperson(s) to the Fellowship Committee.

2.      To appoint a liaison to the Pledge Class Fellowship Committee.

3.      To work with the Finance Committee to ensure that fellowship funds are available and there is a reserve for the next term.

4.      To prepare a record of fellowship activities and information to be passed to later Fellowship Vice Presidents.

 

Clause 5: Pledge Parent(s)

 

1.      To give to all Pledges the opportunity to learn the fundamentals of Alpha Phi Omega and grasp the spirit of this Fraternity before joining.

2.      To have charge of Pledge meetings and the election of Pledge Class officers.

3.      To oversee the execution of Pledge and Active activities concerning the Pledging and Activation Rituals and all surrounding activities.

4.      To have charge of materials to be used in all Pledge Rituals, and Rush.

5.      To appoint Rush, big bro/little bro, and Ritual chairperson(s).

6.      To present the Chapter with any major prospective changes in the Pledge training method for approval before implementation.

7.      To prepare a record of their term for use by later Pledge Parents.

 

Clause 6: Administrative VP

 

1.      To make a written record of the proceedings at all Chapter Meetings.

2.      To prepare an outline agenda for all Chapter Meetings.

3.      To keep an accurate record of the attendance at all Chapter Meetings.

4.      To work with the Membership Vice President(s) to compile and update an Active roster.

5.      To appoint liaisons to the other Section One Chapters, and to oversee their reports to the Chapter.

6.      To direct letters of appreciation on Chapter stationary to persons and organizations who have cooperated with Chapter projects.

7.      To keep an up-to-date record of present addresses and phone numbers of all Chapter alumni, Sectional, Regional, and National staff, and other Chapters.

8.      To formulate and execute a system for encouraging the participation of alumni and other Chapters in assisting with Chi projects.

 

Clause 7: Finance VP

 

1.      To serve as chairperson(s) of the Finance Committee.

2.      To receive and deposit all general Chapter funds and to keep an accurate record thereof.

3.      To make, under proper Chapter budget approval, payments from Chapter accounts by check (checks must be countersigned by another Active Member and a receipt for all services/materials purchased must be obtained.)

4.      To be diligent in the collection of Membership dues, National fees, and Chapter assessments.

5.      To keep an accurate written record of payments and receipts of all moneys and to supply written receipt when asked.

6.      To see that all National fees are promptly transmitted to the National Office by the Membership Vice President(s).

7.      To see that the Chapter budget is prepared and approved by the 3rd Chapter Meeting of the year.

8.      To audit the financial books of the Chapter before turning over to a new treasurer and to prepare tax forms under the guidance of the Advisory Committee during the spring term.

9.      To submit a financial report showing the Chapter’s financial condition bimonthly at a Chapter Meeting.

10.  To be in charge of the planning and implementation of fund-raisers to replenish the Chapter fund.

11.  To appoint a fund raising chairperson and an executor of the Leadership development fund.

 

Clause 8: Historian(s)

 

1.      To serve as chairperson(s) of the Historian Committee.

2.      To prepare a Chapter history for the term, noting significant changes in policy and reasons for changes.

3.      To prepare a Chapter scrapbook.

4.      To be responsible for perpetuation and promotion of the Chapter awards ceremony and the preservation of the Chapter traditions.

 

Clause 9: Sergeant-at-Arms

 

1.      To maintain order at Chapter Meetings and Executive Committee meetings.

2.      To have knowledge of parliamentary procedures.

3.      To be responsible for collecting outstanding dues.

4.      To conduct voting procedures and counting the votes.

5.      Responsible for collecting “donations” from Actives and associate Members not wearing their pins.

 

Clause 10: Interchapter Chair

  1. To promote and facilitate Chapter involvement in interchapter, Sectional, Regional, and National Fraternity events.
  2. To coordinate activities between chapters.

 

Section 8: Duties of Advisors include (but are not limited to) the following:

 

Clause 1: Faculty Advisors

 

1.      To serve as a Member of the Advisory Committee.

2.      To serve as a special Advisor to a committee upon appointment by the chairman of the Advisory Committee.

 

Clause 2: Scouting/Community Advisors

 

1.      To serve as a Member of the Advisory Committee.

2.      To advise the Chapter regarding Scouting activities.

3.      To assist in contacting former Scouts as prospective Members.

4.      To assist the Chapter with projects of interest to the Scouting movement.

 

Clause 3: Additional Advisors

 

1.      Additional Advisors may be appointed by the Fraternity to assist with general or specific Fraternity functions, they shall be responsible for such assistance and be Members of the Advisory Committee.

 

 

 

ARTICLE V: COMMITTEES

 

Section 1: Executive Committee shall . . .

 

Clause 1: be chaired by the President.

 

Clause 2: consist of all elected student officers and the chairs of any standing committee.

 

Clause 3: provide for interpretation of this Constitution.

 

Clause 4: meet at least once a week during the school year.  Special meetings may be held at the discretion of the Chapter President, or upon written request of the majority of Members of the Executive Committee.

 

Clause 5: hold open or closed meetings at the discretion of the Chapter President.  Executive Committee meetings may never be closed to Advisors or Sectional, Regional, or National representatives.

 

Clause 6: formulate a program at the beginning of each term, which shall be subject to approval by the Active Membership at a meeting where a quorum prevails.  This program shall include a tentative outline of Chapter events for the term.

 

Chapter 7: work with the Finance Committee to formulate a yearly term budget.

 

Section 2: Service Committee shall . . .

 

Clause 1: be chaired by the Service Vice President(s)

 

Clause 2: formulate and execute the Chapter Service program.

 

Clause 3: solicit suggestions from Members, students, campus organizations, faculty, college officials, community Members and organizations, and Advisors for possible new service projects to the campus, community, nation, and Fraternity.

 

Clause 4: make plans to carry out “traditional” service projects

 

Clause 5: present suggestions for new types of projects to the Executive Committee or general Membership for approval

 

Clause 6: prepare a detailed written evaluation of service projects and the Service term at the end of the term.

 

Clause 7: assist and be assisted by the Publicity Committee when publicizing service events.

 

 

 

Section 3: Membership Committee shall . . .

 

Clause 1: be chaired by the Membership Vice President(s).

 

Clause 2: formulate and execute the Active Membership program.

 

Clause 3: insure that all National and Chapter policies and requirements are met.

 

Clause 4: work with the President to assist Members in making sectional, regional, and National convention plans.

 

Clause 5: work closely with the leadership development fund executor and the Finance Committee in effort to raise funds for the leadership development fund.

 

Clause 6: work to increase Active retention.

 

Clause 7: work to increase knowledge of Sectional, Regional, and National events.

 

Clause 8: assist in the establishment and/or reactivation of Chapters of Alpha Phi Omega at other colleges and universities.

 

Clause 9: determine Active requirements which shall include but not be limited to service hours and/or number of service projects for a given term.  Any changes in requirements from the previous term must be approved by a simple majority of Members at a regular Chapter Meeting where a quorum prevails.

 

Section 4: Fellowship Committee shall . . .

 

Clause 1: be chaired by the Fellowship Vice President(s).

 

Clause 2: formulate and execute the Chapter Fellowship program.

 

Clause 3: work with the Service Committee to coordinate fellowship events at service projects.

 

Clause 4: coordinate Chapter banquets.

 

Section 5: Finance Committee shall . . .

 

Clause 1: be chaired by the Finance VP

 

Clause 2: formulate a term and yearly budget with the assistance of the Executive Committee to be approved by the Chapter.

 

Clause 3: formulate the term budget and include a tentative outline of Chapter income and expenditures for the current term.

 

Clause 4: submit the term budget to the Chapter no later than the 3rd Chapter Meeting of the new term.

 

Clause 5: seek Chapter approval for all expenditures exceeding budgeted amounts at a Chapter Meeting before such expenses may be reimbursed.

 

Clause 6: see to it that the Chapter books are properly audited at the end of the term by a Member other than the Finance VP

 

Clause 7: propose any necessary changes to the Membership dues.  This proposal is subject to approval by a simple majority of Members at a regular Chapter Meeting where a quorum prevails.

 

Section 6: Historian Committee shall . . .

 

Clause 1: be chaired by the Historian(s).

 

Clause 2: formulate and execute the Chapter awards ceremony.

 

Clause 3: prepare a Chapter history for the term.

 

Section 7: Ad-hoc Committees shall . . .

 

Clause 1: be created by the President to fulfill the needs of the Chapter during the term, with approval of the Members.

 

Clause 2: be chaired by a person selected by the President who will serve until the end of the term or until the purpose of the committee has concluded, which ever comes first.

 

Section 8: All Committees

 

Clause 1: All committees shall make recommendations to the Chapter for carrying out their responsibilities.

 

Clause 2: The Active Membership may override the decision of any committee by 2/3 vote at any meeting with a quorum.

 

ARTICE VI: MEETINGS

 

Section 1: General Chapter Meetings shall be open to all interested parties unless quorum prevails.  Only Members may speak at general meetings unless sponsored by a Member.

 

(Note: No partisan or political speeches may be made during general meeting time.)

 

Section 2: Special Meetings

 

A date for special meetings shall be determined by the President upon approval of the Executive Committee within 15 days of such a request.

 

 

ARTICLE VII: CHAPTER BUSINESS

 

Section 1: Chapter business shall be conducted under Robert’s Rules of Order, except as outlined by this Chapter’s Constitution.

 

Section 2: There shall be no proxy voting.  Absentee votes may be delivered to the Administrative or Membership Vice President.

 

Section 3: Absentee votes must be delivered prior to the election, and count only for the first ballot, not for run off elections.

 

Section 4: In Chapter election, a nominee must obtain at least 51% of all votes cast to be elected.  If no nominee receives 51%, there shall be a run off election between the remaining nominees receiving the highest number of votes.

 

Section 5: A Quorum

 

Clause 1: A quorum shall be required in order to conduct any type of Chapter business for which a vote is required by the Constitution.

 

Clause 2: A quorum shall consist of a simple majority of the current Active Members in good standing.

 

Clause 3: When Chapter business has been previously announced at a general meeting or takes place annually, the Chapter may move into a “committee as a whole” to conduct Chapter business without a quorum.  This shall include but not be limited to the voting on the budget and elections.

 

Section 6: Nominations shall be opened at a general Chapter Meeting and remain open for at least 1 week.  All nominations must be seconded in order to be valid.

 

Section 7: Nominations shall be closed no more than 1 day prior to elections.

 

Section 8: Nominations, written or telephoned, will be accepted prior to the close of nominations by the Administrative Vice President or the President from Members who cannot attend regular nominations.

 

Section 9: Nominees shall be notified of their nomination prior to voting (if possible) by the current President.

 

Section 10: Chapter elections shall be held during the beginning of winter quarter for the election of winter/spring officers and during the 9th week of the spring quarter for the election of the summer/fall officers.

 

Section 11: All elections shall be by secret ballot unless there is only 1 candidate (when the Chapter may vote to accept the candidate by acclamation as long as there are no “nay” votes.)  Each Active shall have only 1 vote per ballot.

 

Section 12: No Member may hold 2 elected offices simultaneously or the same office for more than 2 complete and consecutive terms.  (Any officer specially elected more than ½ way through a term shall not be considered to have served an entire term.)

 

 

ARTICE VIII: DUES, FEES, DISTRIBUTIONS, ASSESSMENTS

 

Section 1: All Pledges shall pay a Chapter pledging fee in addition to the National pledging fee.  This fee shall be paid at the time of pledging.

 

Section 2: All Pledges shall pay a Chapter initiation fee in addition to the National initiation fee.  This fee shall be paid at the time of Initiation and Active Membership shall not be formal until it is paid.

 

Section 3: All Members shall pay Membership dues, both National and Chapter, according to their status as Actives, associates, etc.

 

Section 4: Advisory and honorary Members shall be exempt from paying Chapter dues.

 

Section 5: In the event of financial debt incurred by the Chapter, equal assessments may be levied by an affirmative vote of ¾ of the Active Members present at a meeting where a quorum prevails.  All Active Members shall have 21 days to pay these assessments.  There shall be no penalty against anyone unable to pay these assessments who is able to demonstrate financial constraints.

 

Section 6: Associate Member fees must be greater than Active Member fees.

 

Section 7: An Active Member can choose to become an associate Member up until eight weeks into a given term.  There is no time requirement for the changing of associate status to Active status as defined in the Constitution, Article III, Section 4.

 

Section 8: Any changes in Membership status shall be accompanied by the appropriate refund or additional charge of the Membership fee.

 

ARTICLE IX: DSK VOTING

 

Chapter Distinguished Service Key Voting shall be conducted by the Membership Committee at the request of any Active Member.  A ¾ approval of the Actives at a meeting where a quorum prevails shall be required.  All votes shall be taken by secret ballot.

 

 

ARTICLE X: AMENDMENTS

 

Section 1: This Constitution may be amended by a ¾ affirmative vote of the Actives at a general Chapter Meeting where a quorum prevails.

 

Section 2: All proposed amendments to this Constitution must be submitted in writing to the general Chapter Membership at least 2 weeks prior to the meeting at which the vote is to be taken.

 

 

ARTICLE XI: CONSTITUTIONAL RESPONSIBILITY

 

All elected Chapter officers shall have a copy of the Constitution.  The President and Membership VP shall be responsible for seeing to it that each officer and interested Member receives a Constitution, an should also carry their own with them to all scheduled Chapter Meetings, Pledge and Active ceremonies, installations, and awards ceremonies.
